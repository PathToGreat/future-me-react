PHASE 26: Avatar Differentiation Calibration (Body + Face) Across Accounts

Problem:
Across multiple test accounts with intentionally poor metrics, the avatar body composition and facial expression look nearly the same as a high-performing account. This makes the system feel unresponsive.

We are NOT changing UI.
We are NOT changing the overall architecture.
We are calibrating mapping so differences become visibly meaningful while remaining realistic.

Goal:
Bad inputs must create a visibly different body composition and facial expression than good inputs, even before long history exists, without exaggeration.

-----------------------------------------
A) DIAGNOSE WHERE COMPRESSION IS HAPPENING
-----------------------------------------

Add a lightweight internal diagnostic (no console spam in production) to confirm:

- PhysicalCompositionScore per account
- Selected preset tier
- Key body params (waistTaper, shoulderWidth, armThickness, legThickness)
- EmotionalStability / stress-derived values
- facialTension, postureLean, vibrancy, energyGlow
- projectionConfidence tier

This is to verify whether:
1) Scores are too similar (scoring compression)
or
2) Visual mapping is too dampened (mapping compression)

-----------------------------------------
B) INCREASE BODY DIFFERENTIATION WITHOUT EXAGGERATION
-----------------------------------------

Current issue:
PhysicalCompositionScore bands + preset deltas are too compressed, so many users fall into similar-looking tiers.

Fix requirements:
1) Adjust score band thresholds so tiers separate more clearly.
   Example target structure (tunable):
   - 0–30 Overweight
   - 30–50 Soft
   - 50–70 AverageFit
   - 70–85 LeanAthletic
   - 85–100 MuscularAthletic

2) Slightly increase geometric separation between presets, focusing on:
   - waistTaper (most noticeable)
   - shoulderWidth (second)
   - armThickness and legThickness (third)
   - chestSize and hipWidth (subtle)

3) Preserve realism constraints from bodyConstraints.js.

4) Ensure female presets remain capable of muscular/lean outcomes (no suppression).

Success:
An intentionally “bad” account lands in Soft/Overweight and looks meaningfully different than Muscular/Lean accounts.

-----------------------------------------
C) MAKE FACE STATES MORE READABLE
-----------------------------------------

Current issue:
Facial expression is too neutral across the middle range.

Fix requirements:
1) Increase expressiveness slightly, but stay subtle.
2) Ensure stress scale remains correct: 1 calm → 5 very stressed.
3) Expand differentiation especially between:
   - calm (stress 1–2) → relaxed eyes/brows/mouth
   - neutral (stress ~3) → neutral mouth, normal brows
   - stressed (stress 4–5 or low emotional stability) → mild brow tension, flatter mouth

4) Keep “no shame” tone: never angry, never grotesque, never sickly.

Success:
A calm account looks calm, a stressed account looks meaningfully tenser.

-----------------------------------------
D) PROJECTION CONFIDENCE SHOULD NOT FLATTEN CURRENT VIEW
-----------------------------------------

Important:
ProjectionConfidence gating is correct for FUTURE deltas, but CURRENT avatar should reflect CURRENT inputs.

Fix requirement:
1) Ensure projectionConfidence dampening only affects FUTURE projection deltas.
2) Current avatar should always use present mapping without being capped by confidence tiers.
3) Future avatar still respects LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH gating.

Success:
A new user with poor CURRENT metrics still looks meaningfully different in CURRENT view. FUTURE remains guarded.

-----------------------------------------
E) TEST MATRIX (REQUIRED)
-----------------------------------------

Create a simple test harness validation (can be dev-only) with 4 synthetic profiles:

1) Excellent:
   activity=5 nutrition=5 consistency=high sleep=4 stress=1–2
2) Average:
   activity=3 nutrition=3 consistency=medium sleep=3 stress=3
3) Poor:
   activity=1–2 nutrition=1–2 consistency=low sleep=2 stress=4
4) Mixed:
   activity=5 nutrition=2 consistency=high sleep=2 stress=4

For each profile, confirm:
- Tier selection differs appropriately
- Body params differ visibly but realistically
- Face expression differs appropriately
- Male and female both behave correctly

-----------------------------------------
FILES LIKELY TO CHANGE
-----------------------------------------
avatarPresets.js (preset deltas)
mapTraitsToAvatarParams.js (thresholds + mapping calibration)
projectionConfidence.js or usage sites (ensure only FUTURE is gated)
HumanAvatarRenderer.jsx (only if face feature mapping needs tiny adjustment)

-----------------------------------------
SUCCESS CRITERIA
-----------------------------------------

1) Good vs bad accounts produce clearly different bodies in CURRENT view.
2) Calm vs stressed accounts produce clearly different faces.
3) FUTURE view remains confidence-gated.
4) No UI changes.
5) No regressions to constraints or gender robustness.
6) Build passes cleanly.

Return:
- What was causing the similarity (scores vs mapping vs gating)
- Updated tier bands
- Updated preset deltas summary
- Confirmation CURRENT is not dampened by projectionConfidence
- Test matrix results
