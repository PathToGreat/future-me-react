Trend Intelligence Patterns
1. Stress-Stability Pattern
Trigger: Stress rolling average decreases after at least 5 days of stable sleep improvement.
Card Text:
“Your stress stabilizes only after sleep improves consistently for several days.”
2. Sleep-Recovery Pattern
Trigger: Sleep quality or duration consistently improves following days with reduced movement or rest periods.
Card Text:
“Your sleep quality shows improvement following consistent rest or lighter movement days.”
3. Movement-Buffer Pattern
Trigger: High activity days correlate with smaller stress spikes or better sleep quality.
Card Text:
“Movement appears to buffer poor sleep and stress more effectively than nutrition alone.”
4. Consistency-Decay Pattern
Trigger: Metrics show decline after 2+ consecutive days of missing logs or inconsistent behavior.
Card Text:
“Consistency drops after two consecutive days without stable logging or routine activity.”
5. Nutrition-Impact Pattern
Trigger: Changes in nutrition metrics correlate with small but observable shifts in energy or focus.
Card Text:
“Changes in your nutrition habits are associated with subtle shifts in energy and focus.”
6. Focus-Stability Pattern
Trigger: Focus zone remains steady across 5+ days and correlates with improvement in overall metrics.
Card Text:
“Progress accelerates when your focus zone remains stable for several consecutive days.”
7. Stress-Lag Pattern
Trigger: Stress spikes precede drops in other metrics by 1–2 days.
Card Text:
“Stress spikes appear to precede small declines in other daily metrics.”
8. Recovery-Slope Pattern
Trigger: Metrics show faster improvement after a period of plateau.
Card Text:
“Your recent improvements occurred more quickly following a period of stability.”
9. Multi-Metric Correlation Pattern
Trigger: Two or more metrics show a repeatable relationship over the last 28 days.
Card Text:
“Your sleep and stress levels consistently move together over the last month.”
10. Early-Signal Pattern
Trigger: First detectable improvement in any metric after 3–7 days of logging.
Card Text:
“You’ve started to show your first measurable change in this area.”
11. Plateau Detection Pattern
Trigger: Metrics remain stable for more than 7 consecutive days.
Card Text:
“Your metrics have remained consistent over the past week, indicating a stable trend.”
12. Focus-Drift Pattern
Trigger: Focus zone changes frequently (3+ changes in a 7-day window) and coincides with metric volatility.
Card Text:
“Frequent changes in your focus zone correspond with more variable metric results.”
13. Peak-Effect Pattern
Trigger: Maximum metric values in a week correlate with prior behavior patterns.
Card Text:
“Your peak performance aligns with consistent habits earlier in the week.”
14. Cross-Impact Pattern
Trigger: One metric improves while another declines, showing compensatory behavior.
Card Text:
“Improvements in one area appear linked to small declines in another.”
15. Momentum Pattern
Trigger: Sequential improvement across multiple metrics over 7+ days.
Card Text:
“Multiple metrics are improving together over the past week, showing a clear momentum trend.”
Usage Notes for Replit
Trigger thresholds: Define per metric (rolling average delta, minimum days of data, effect size).
Card frequency: Maximum 2 per user per week; skip if no pattern meets threshold.
UI placement: Below NoticingCard, above DailyReasonToReturn or prompts.
Tone: Neutral, reflective, evidence-first. Never advice, encouragement, or prescriptive.
Metrics to log: Pattern surfaced rate, dismiss vs revisited, return after insight.
