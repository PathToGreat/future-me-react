We are restructuring the Home Dashboard for clarity, retention, and emotional impact.
Current problem:
There are multiple small rectangular insight popups at the top of the screen (Current Focus, Observed Change, Pattern Detected, etc.). This creates visual fragmentation and weakens psychological impact.
Goal:
Replace these multiple top-of-screen insight rectangles with ONE dominant card called:
“Today’s Reflection”
This card should:
Pull the highest priority insight dynamically from:
Pattern detected
Observed change
Current focus
Trend insight
Only display ONE insight at a time.
Rotate daily OR when a more meaningful insight becomes available.
Be visually larger and calmer than current small rectangles.
Include:
Icon (subtle, not emoji-heavy)
Headline (short, strong)
One supporting sentence
Optional expandable “Learn More” section
Design Requirements:
Clean
Minimal
No gamified language
No confetti
No overstimulation
Should feel reflective, not promotional
Example structure:
Title: Today’s Reflection
Headline:
Your stress stabilizes when sleep improves consistently.
Supporting:
When sleep becomes consistent for several days, your recovery system regulates more efficiently.
Expandable Section (optional):
Why this matters:
Sustained sleep consistency lowers baseline stress load and improves emotional resilience.
Below this card:
Move remaining insights into a new section called:
“Recent Observations”
This section should:
Be collapsible
Show historical insights
Not compete visually with Today’s Reflection
Remove all multiple small insight rectangles from the top.
Visual hierarchy of Home should now be:
Mini Avatar Preview (we will implement in Phase 2)
Today’s Reflection (dominant card)
Direction Indicator (we will implement in Phase 3)
Weekly Trend Summary
Quick Log Button
Secondary sections
The emotional tone must feel:
Grounded.
Intelligent.
Calm.
Insight-driven.
Not dopamine-driven.
Do not alter avatar engine logic.
Do not alter scoring logic.
Only restructure dashboard hierarchy and insight presentation.
Implement using existing React structure and state logic.
