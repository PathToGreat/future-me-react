Implement Phase 4: Upgrade insight depth and usefulness while staying calm, non-gamified, and brief.
Current state:
TodaysReflection.jsx selects one highest-priority insight (pattern detected, observed change, daily insight, focus zone, fallback). Each insight may include an expandable “Why this matters” section.
Goal:
Make “Why this matters” consistently available for every TodaysReflection insight type, and make it context-aware based on which metrics/zones are implicated.
Rules:
No emojis.
No praise language.
No shame language.
No hype.
No “crush it” tone.
Keep the Why text to 1–2 sentences max.
The Why must teach a cause-effect relationship that increases awareness and return behavior.
Deliverables:
Create an InsightExplanationMap (or similar) that returns:
headline
supporting sentence
whyThisMatters (1–2 sentences)
for each insight type:
a) PATTERN_DETECTED
b) OBSERVED_CHANGE
c) DAILY_INSIGHT
d) CURRENT_FOCUS
e) CONTINUITY_FALLBACK
Context awareness:
For each insight, tailor “whyThisMatters” to the implicated metric(s):
Sleep
Stress
Activity
Nutrition
Consistency
Examples of acceptable “Why this matters” lines (use similar tone, not necessarily exact text):
Sleep: “Sleep consistency stabilizes recovery signals, which makes energy and stress regulation more predictable.”
Stress: “Lower baseline stress improves decision quality and makes healthy habits easier to sustain.”
Activity: “Steady movement supports mood regulation and improves sleep pressure at night.”
Nutrition: “Stable nutrition reduces energy volatility and supports recovery.”
Consistency: “Consistency reduces friction. When the routine is predictable, change requires less willpower.”
Stress inversion reminder:
When stress improves (lower), explain it as stability and recovery, not “winning.”
Improve the “Learn More” / expansion behavior:
Default view: show headline + one supporting sentence.
Expansion: reveals “Why this matters” and optionally one “Try this” micro-suggestion that is also calm and non-prescriptive.
The “Try this” suggestion should be optional and short (one line), and only included when relevant and safe.
Examples:
Sleep focus: “Try keeping wake time within a 60-minute window for a few days.”
Stress focus: “Try a short decompression block after work before family time.”
Activity focus: “Try a 10-minute walk earlier in the day.”
Nutrition focus: “Try keeping the first meal consistent for a few days.”
Consistency: “Try logging at the same time each day to reduce friction.”
No medical claims. No absolute promises.
Make sharing reflect the philosophy:
If “Share this reflection” exists, ensure it shares the short reflection (headline + supporting sentence) and does NOT share score or streak language.
Ensure the Today’s Reflection remains the dominant card, and the expansion does not push the rest of the dashboard too far down. Keep spacing tight and clean.
Do not alter:
Avatar engine logic
Scoring logic
DirectionIndicator thresholds
Only improve the insight copy system and ensure consistent depth.
Implementation notes:
Keep the mapping system simple and maintainable.
If you already have insight objects, enrich them with whyThisMatters and optional tryThis fields at creation time.
Testing checklist:
Each insight type shows an expandable “Why this matters.”
The text changes depending on which metric(s) are involved.
Tone remains calm and descriptive.
No emojis, praise, hype, or shame.
