Future Me — the honest gap:

The mockup shows a hyper-realistic 3D character that looks like the same person, just physically transformed. That's the hardest part and where things split into two real paths:

Path A — What we have now (SVG avatar):
The current "Future Me" is a custom SVG figure that changes body shape, posture, facial tension, and expression based on metrics. It's data-driven and responsive, but it's a generic figure — not personalized to the user's face or appearance. Nowhere close to the mockup's Future Me visually.

Path B — AI-generated image (closest to the mockup):
Using an image generation API (OpenAI, Replicate, Stability AI), we could take the user's uploaded photo and generate a photorealistic "future version" of them based on their projected trajectory — improved body composition, posture, expression. The result would be a realistic photo-style image. The tricky part is face preservation — standard image generators don't guarantee the face stays the same person. There are specialized models (like InstantID on Replicate) that do a better job of this, but it's not perfect.

Path C — Hybrid approach (pragmatic middle ground):
Show the user's actual photo on the left (Current Me), and on the right show an AI-generated image prompted with their trajectory data. The likeness won't be exact, but it would be photorealistic and directionally meaningful. This is likely the most achievable version close to the mockup.
